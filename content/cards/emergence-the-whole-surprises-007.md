## Idea
When components interact, the system can exhibit behaviors that **none of the individual components have**. This isn't magic -- it's a consequence of the interaction rules. But you can't predict it by studying parts in isolation.

## Example
Each neuron in your brain is simple: receive signals, fire or don't. But 86 billion of them interacting produce consciousness, creativity, humor. No single neuron is conscious. Consciousness is an emergent property of their interaction.

## Probe
A traffic jam can form even when no one slows down first -- just from cars being slightly too close. The jam is "real" (you sit in it) but has no physical substance. It's a pattern in the flow. What other emergent things feel real but have no physical form?
