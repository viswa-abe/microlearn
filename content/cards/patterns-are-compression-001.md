## Idea
A pattern is anything that lets you **predict what comes next** without seeing it. The more accurately you can predict, the deeper the pattern you've found. This is literally what compression algorithms do -- find patterns to make data smaller.

## Example
The sequence `2, 4, 6, 8, ...` -- you instantly know the next number is 10. Your brain compressed the rule to "add 2." But `2, 4, 6, 8, 11, ...` breaks the pattern. That discomfort you feel? That's your pattern recognizer screaming.

## Probe
A random sequence like `7, 3, 9, 1, 5, ...` has no pattern -- which means it *can't be compressed*. Does this mean randomness contains the most information? (Surprisingly, yes -- this is a real result in information theory.)
