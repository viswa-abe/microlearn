## Idea
When the output of a process feeds back as input, you get a loop. **Positive feedback amplifies** (microphone -> speaker -> microphone = screech). **Negative feedback stabilizes** (thermostat detects heat -> turns off heater -> cools -> turns on). Most complex systems are tangles of both.

## Example
Social media: you post -> get likes -> feel good -> post more -> get more likes. That's positive feedback -- it amplifies. But there's also negative feedback: post too much -> people unfollow -> fewer likes -> you post less.

## Probe
A bank run is pure positive feedback: one person withdraws -> others panic -> more withdraw -> more panic. What would a negative feedback mechanism look like that could stop it? (This is literally what deposit insurance was designed to be.)
