## Idea
The deepest patterns aren't about what changes -- they're about **what stays the same while everything else changes**. These are called invariants, and finding them is the master skill in both math and engineering.

## Example
Shuffle a deck of cards however you like. The number of cards stays at 52. That's an invariant. Now: can you shuffle so that every red card has a black card next to it? *Knowing the invariant (52 cards, 26 red, 26 black) constrains what's possible.*

## Probe
In a chess game, pieces move, get captured, positions change constantly. But what's an invariant? (Hint: think about the color of the square a bishop sits on.)
