## Idea
Every system has **stocks** (accumulations -- things you can count at a moment) and **flows** (rates -- things you measure per unit time). Confusion between stocks and flows is the #1 cause of bad reasoning about systems.

## Example
A bathtub. **Stock:** water in the tub (gallons). **Inflow:** faucet (gallons/minute). **Outflow:** drain (gallons/minute). The water level rises when inflow > outflow. Obvious here -- but people confuse stock and flow constantly with money, population, CO2.

## Probe
National debt is a *stock*. The deficit is a *flow*. If the deficit drops from $1T to $500B, is the debt going up or down? (Up -- the deficit is still positive, so the stock is still growing, just slower.)
