## Idea
When there's a **delay between action and effect**, systems overshoot. You keep pushing because you haven't seen the result yet. Then the result arrives all at once, so you overcorrect. This creates oscillation -- swinging back and forth.

## Example
A shower with slow pipes. You turn the hot knob -- nothing happens. Turn more. Still cold. Turn more. Suddenly: scalding. You crank it cold. Wait. Still hot. Crank more. Then: freezing. The delay between your action and the temperature change causes you to oscillate.

## Probe
Central banks raise interest rates to cool inflation. But rate changes take 12-18 months to fully affect the economy. How does this delay make monetary policy inherently oscillatory? (And what does the Fed do to compensate?)
