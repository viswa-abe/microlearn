## Idea
Probability isn't about coins and dice -- it's a **language for reasoning under uncertainty**. The core move: you have a belief (prior), you see evidence, you update. This is Bayes' theorem, and it's how rational thinking works.

## Example
You hear hoofbeats. Prior belief: probably horses (you're in Texas). But you're at the zoo. Updated belief: maybe zebras. You see black-and-white stripes through the fence. Updated again: almost certainly zebras. Each piece of evidence shifts your belief.

## Probe
A medical test is 99% accurate. You test positive for a disease that affects 1 in 10,000 people. What's the chance you actually have it? (It's much lower than 99% -- work through why. This is the most common probability mistake people make.)
